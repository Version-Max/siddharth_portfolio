About Me
I am an avid learner. I like to learn through the medium of talking, traveling, reading be it the news or magazine or books, and documentaries. I am interested in a world where we all eventually grow diverse intellectually.
With the advent of portable devices, the internet, and the art of tapping into the raw data to convert it to knowledge we can get to world that is intellectually diverse. I am interested in forwarding this journey with contributing my work experience, my education, and my general quest for learning.
How can I contribute to the world?
I have no doubt that if everyone is educated, and given the tools to discover and develop knowledge then it will be a perfect leap for humanity to a better world.
Just making books, schools or the internet accessible alone is not enough to get to this vision. A huge onus lays on finding custom teachers. Every person learns the same topics in different ways, one can learn a complex math problem by just seeing the equation while people like me would learn by seeing examples. I have personally come to learn a lot of topics that I was bad at in school thanks to YouTube. This is just one example where I found people whose teaching methods I can relate to and thus understand these topics pretty well. I am confident this culture of sharing and learning would grow many folds. I have seen the benefit first hand of this type of knowledge sharing, and therefore, I would in long run try to contribute knowledge sharing at no cost through different mediums and diverse teachers.
My Story
I have been lucky to explore the world as a kid. Before moving to America, I have spent time in the UK, and briefly in Switzerland. This journey has made it fair for myself to be deemed a little nomadic. This early exposure to diverse worlds was a good push to get out of my comfort zone early on and be open to new experiences. Not only am I open but I have actively seeked new experiences be it through travel, books, or just talking to a stranger on the bus. Learning about the world through different points of view is a habit I can highly credit to my childhood.
Growing up at a time where the technology has been changing at light speed made me want to study technology in college. Therefore, I picked computer science in my undergrad as the major of my choice. Right after I got the lucky break to work for my alma mater itself. Working at a University setting comes with its own challenges and opportunities. The challenges I saw was to operate and solve complex problems all on my own. This challenge was rightly an opportunity to grow to understand the world of data analytics, building communication skills, and managing projects. As I said I did not operate with teams therefore, I had to learn on the job. I began creating ad hoc tools for data analysis, summoned statistics to help marketing directors with A/B testing, implemented some Machine Learning models to prediction and classification, and many wild idea approaches to solve dynamic problems.
The above experience highly encouraged me to seek a masters degree where I could properly get trained in the data realm field. Therefore, I enrolled myself at DePaul university to study Business Intelligence. The benefit of a major like this was to learn from actual industry professionals who were our instructors, understand the world of data with focus on all fields such as Machine Learning, Statistics, Data Science, and Databases. An exposure of all these areas is what has made me confident, comprehensive and excited in using data to solve complex problems.
